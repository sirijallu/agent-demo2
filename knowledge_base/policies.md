# Wanderly Travel Policies

Demo policy knowledge base for the Wanderly travel agent. These are illustrative
policies for a fictional agency, not real terms of service.

## Cancellation & Refund Policy

### Free cancellation window

All bookings made through Wanderly can be cancelled free of charge within 12
hours of booking, as long as the departure date is at least 7 days away. The
full amount, including taxes and fees, is refunded to the original payment
method.

### Standard cancellation tiers

After the 12-hour window, cancellation fees depend on how far in advance of
departure you cancel:

- 60 or more days before departure: 90% refund of the trip cost.
- 30–59 days before departure: 75% refund.
- 14–29 days before departure: 50% refund.
- 7–13 days before departure: 25% refund.
- Fewer than 7 days before departure: no refund, except where required by law
  or covered by travel insurance.

Flight tickets booked as part of a package follow the airline's fare rules,
which may be stricter than the tiers above. Basic economy fares are generally
non-refundable after the 24-hour window.

### Refund processing time

Approved refunds are issued to the original payment method within 5–10
business days. Bank processing may add 1–2 billing cycles for credit card
statements.

### Changes instead of cancellation

Date or destination changes made 30 or more days before departure incur a
$75 per-traveler change fee plus any fare difference. Within 30 days of
departure, changes are treated as a cancellation and rebooking under the
tiers above.

### Airline- or weather-initiated cancellations

If an airline cancels a flight, or a government travel advisory or natural
disaster makes the destination unsafe, travelers may choose between a full
refund or free rebooking, regardless of the tiers above. Wanderly waives all
agency fees in these cases.

### How to cancel

Cancellations can be made from the "My Trips" page or by contacting the
Wanderly concierge. The cancellation timestamp used for fee calculation is
when the request is received, not when it is processed.

## Baggage Policy

### Carry-on allowance

Every ticket booked through Wanderly includes one personal item (max 40 x 30 x
15 cm, must fit under the seat). Standard and premium fares also include one
carry-on bag (max 55 x 40 x 23 cm, up to 10 kg / 22 lb). Basic economy fares
on some partner airlines do not include a carry-on bag — this is flagged at
booking time.

### Checked baggage allowance

- Economy: first checked bag included on international itineraries (max 23 kg
  / 50 lb); domestic itineraries typically charge $35 for the first bag.
- Premium economy: two checked bags included (23 kg each).
- Business/first: two checked bags included (32 kg / 70 lb each).

### Excess and overweight fees

- Overweight (23–32 kg): $75–$100 per bag, per direction, depending on the
  airline.
- Oversized (linear dimensions over 158 cm / 62 in): $100–$200 per bag.
- Additional bags beyond the allowance: $100–$150 per bag on international
  routes.

Exact fees are set by the operating airline; Wanderly shows the airline's fee
table on the booking confirmation page.

### Special items

Sports equipment (skis, golf bags, surfboards), musical instruments, and
mobility equipment can be added to a booking through the concierge. Wheelchairs
and other mobility devices always travel free. Most airlines count sports
equipment as one checked bag if it is within weight limits; oversized fees may
apply to surfboards over 2 m.

### Delayed, damaged, or lost baggage

Report issues to the airline's baggage desk before leaving the airport and
keep the file reference number. Airlines are liable for checked baggage under
the Montreal Convention (up to roughly $1,700 per passenger). Wanderly's
concierge can help travelers file claims, and trips with Wanderly travel
insurance include additional baggage-delay compensation (see insurance
section).

## Travel Insurance Policy

### Included protection

Every Wanderly package booking includes basic protection at no extra cost:

- Trip interruption due to airline cancellation: rebooking assistance and up
  to $300 in incidental expenses (meals, ground transport).
- Baggage delay over 12 hours: up to $100 for essentials.

### Wanderly TravelSafe (optional plan)

TravelSafe can be added to any booking up to 48 hours before departure. It
costs 5–7% of the total trip price, depending on destination and traveler age,
and includes:

- Trip cancellation for covered reasons (illness or injury of a traveler or
  immediate family member, jury duty, military orders, job loss after 1+ year
  of employment): reimbursement of non-refundable trip costs up to $10,000
  per traveler.
- Emergency medical coverage abroad: up to $100,000, including hospital
  stays and prescribed medication.
- Emergency medical evacuation and repatriation: up to $250,000.
- Trip delay over 6 hours: up to $200 per day, max $600.
- Baggage loss: up to $2,500 per traveler beyond airline liability.
- Baggage delay over 6 hours: up to $300 for essentials.

### Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR) upgrade

The CFAR upgrade can be added to TravelSafe within 48 hours of the original
booking. It costs an additional 3–4% of the trip price and reimburses 75% of
non-refundable trip costs when cancelling for any reason at least 48 hours
before departure — no covered reason required.

### Key exclusions

TravelSafe does not cover:

- Pre-existing medical conditions, unless the plan was purchased within 14
  days of the initial trip deposit (which activates the pre-existing condition
  waiver).
- Injuries from extreme sports (skydiving, BASE jumping, mountaineering above
  4,500 m, cave diving). Standard recreational activities such as snorkeling,
  skiing on marked runs, and zip-lining are covered.
- Losses caused by intoxication or illegal activity.
- Travel to destinations under a "Do Not Travel" government advisory issued
  before booking.

### How to file a claim

Claims are filed online through the TravelSafe portal within 90 days of the
incident. Required documentation typically includes receipts, medical records
for health claims, and airline or police reports where applicable. Claims are
usually processed within 15 business days of receiving complete documentation.
